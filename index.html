A little bit about me...

Becoming a software developer was a moon shot. During my undergrad in Health & Enterprise at the University of Oklahoma I began to hear about the developing tech scene and immediately became interested. Learning to code became a hobby after college, and fit well with my desire to learn new skills like Adobe Creative Suite and Logic Pro X.

It wasn't until after I started my career as an Assistant Manager for a blood plasma center that I really began to see my need to program. I would come across analysis issues I just couldn't solve with Excel. I noticed how the company could increase production or market value more effectively if we had a simple application. My desire deepened, I tried to learn to code on my own... but the pace was slow and schools like Codecademy and CodeSchool could only take me so far.

Enter The Iron Yard Academy. My previous position relocated me to Durham, NC. Lucky for me the Triangle is on the up-and-up in Tech and has an amazing community. I heard about TIY and knew I had to go. After long nights and a few frustrating coding vacations...yes, I took vacations to learn to code... I knew what I needed to do. I quit my bread-n-butter...took a risk... and am proud to say that I'm a Developer. Take a look below and see what I've accomplished.

Question Box

Question Box was my first full django app that utilized a RESTful API. The website allows users to post questions and have users also answer and be upvoted/downvoted. Think StackOverflow. This was a partner project also. I learned about django-restframeworks and increased knowledge of Views and "MVC" in general.

Git Repo

Movie Rater

Movie Rater uses pandas and numpy to analyze users movie preferences and classify them based on similar characteristics. We could match them up with other users and recommend movies based on "matches" seen and unseen movies. I learned how to use SciKit learn and deepened knowledge of classification and machine learning through this project.

Git Repo

Honey-nut Clusters

Honey-nut Clusters was a project were I used pandas and SciKit-learn's machine learning to classify untested cereals into 4 groups. I learned how to make a test sample and train the machine. I also learned how to build pipelines and use the KMeans algorithm. Through this simple project my understanding of statistical analysis grew as well as my excitement for this tool and it's applications.
